One post-doc position in ICT on the research project \r\n\r\n"Advanced SMT Techniques for Word-level Formal Verification - (WOLF)"\r\n\r\nis available in Trento, Italy, under the joint supervision of \r\n- Alessandro Cimatti, FBK, Trento, and\r\n- Roberto Sebastiani, DISI, University of Trento.\r\n\r\nThe research activity will be carried out jointly within the Embedded\r\nSystems (ES) Research Unit of the Center for Scientific and\r\nTechnological Research of the Fondazione Bruno Kessler (FBK), Trento,\r\nand the Software Engineering, Formal Methods & Security  Research\r\nProgram, at Department of Information Engineering and Computer Science\r\n(DISI) of University of Trento.\r\n\r\nAim and Scope\r\n=============\r\n \r\nThe research activity will aim at investigating and developing novel\r\ntechniques, methodologies and support tools for Satisfiability Modulo\r\nTheories (SMT) for the formal verification of systems.  This work will\r\nbe part of the "Advanced SMT Techniques for Word-level Formal\r\nVerification - (WOLF)" project, a three-year research project\r\nsupported by SRC/GRC (http://www.src.org/compete/s201113/), in strict\r\ncollaboration with the Formal Verification Group at Intel, Haifa, and\r\nother major HW companies.\r\n\r\nThe ultimate goal of the WOLF project is to provide a comprehensive\r\nSMT package to support effective formal verification of systems\r\nranging from RTL circuits all the way up to high-level hardware\r\ndescription languages (e.g. SystemC) and software. The package will be\r\nimplemented on top of the MathSAT SMT platform\r\n(http://mathsat.fbk.eu/), and provided as an API.\r\n\r\nCandidate Profile\r\n=================\r\n\r\nThe ideal candidate should have an PhD in computer science or related\r\ndiscipline, and combine solid theoretical background and excellent\r\nsoftware development skills (in particular C/C++).\r\n\r\nA solid background knowledge and/or previous experience on\r\nSatisfiability Modulo Theory (SMT) and/or \r\nPropositional Satisfiability (SAT) is required. \r\nPrevious experience in the following areas will also be considered\r\nfavourably: Model Checking, Automated Reasoning, Constraint Solving\r\nand Optimization, Embedded Systems Design Languages (e.g. Verilog,\r\nVHDL).\r\n\r\nThe candidate should be able to work in a collaborative environment,\r\nwith a strong committment to reaching research excellence and achieving\r\nassigned objectives.\r\n\r\nTerms and dates\r\n===============\r\n\r\nThe position will start as soon as possible, and will have to be\r\nrenewed yearly, for a maximum of three years.
